## L1 : About introduction to the NLP:
> In this lecture, I understood what Natural Language Processing (NLP) is and why it is important for machines to understand human language.

- NLP is a field that lies at the intersection of **Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Linguistics**.
- The main goal of NLP is to enable machines to **read, understand, interpret, and generate human language**.
- Human language is **ambiguous, context-dependent, and probabilistic**, which makes NLP challenging.
- Unlike programming languages, natural languages do not follow strict rules.

---

### Probabilistic View of Language

> In this lecture, I got to know about the **probabilistic nature of language** and why probability is important in NLP.

- Language can be modeled using **probability distributions**.
- The probability of a word depends on the **previous words (context)**.
- Example:  
  > *"I am going to the ___"*  
  The next word can be *market, school, office*, etc., each with a probability.
- This idea leads to **Language Models**, which predict the next word based on context.

---

### Challenges in NLP

> This lecture helped me understand why NLP is difficult compared to other AI problems.

- **Ambiguity**: Same word can have multiple meanings (e.g., *bank*).
- **Context dependency**: Meaning of a sentence changes based on context.
- **Syntax vs Semantics**: Grammatically correct sentences may still be meaningless.
- **Language diversity**: Different languages, dialects, slang, and informal usage.

---

### Core NLP Tasks

> In this lecture, I learned about basic NLP tasks that form the foundation of advanced models.

- **Tokenization** – splitting text into words or subwords.
- **Part-of-Speech (POS) tagging** – assigning grammatical tags.
- **Named Entity Recognition (NER)** – detecting names, places, dates.
- **Sentiment Analysis** – understanding emotions in text.
- **Machine Translation** – converting text from one language to another.

---

### From NLP to Large Language Models (LLMs)

> This lecture introduced how modern NLP evolved into Large Language Models.

https://youtu.be/RI1YX4xgAAI?si=al1dVUPQjJzJB3E7
- Earlier NLP systems were **rule-based and statistical**.
- Modern NLP uses **deep learning and neural networks**.
- **Large Language Models (LLMs)** learn from massive text data.
- LLMs capture:
  - Grammar
  - Semantics
  - Context
  - World knowledge
- Examples: **GPT, BERT, Transformer-based models**

---

### Key Takeaways

- Language understanding is inherently **probabilistic**.
- Context plays a crucial role in meaning.
- NLP tasks build the foundation for LLMs.
- LLMs represent the **current state-of-the-art** in NLP.
